Method: Chemiluminescent Immunoassay (CLIA)

Interpretation(s):
Rubella virus, the only member of the Rubivirus genus, causes rubella (also known as German measles), an acute exanthematous infection of children and adults. The clinical illness is characterized by rash, fever, and lymphadenopathy and can resemble a mild case of measles. These viruses also cause arthralgias and occasional encephalitis. Infection is particularly disastrous if contracted during the first 4 months of pregnancy. If a nonimmunologically protected woman is infected during pregnancy, a high risk of fetal embryopathy damage may occur.

Test Utility:

IgG antibodies become detectable in a few days after the onset of signs and symptoms and reach peak levels in seven to ten days.
