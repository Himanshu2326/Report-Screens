We understand and accept (as applicable) that:
  1. The treatment involves the use of expensive hormonal medications to stimulate the ovaries for production of a number of eggs (Oocytes) and regular scanning and blood tests for monitoring patients' response, which may vary from person to person.
  2. The drugs that are used to stimulate the ovaries to raise Oocytes have temporary side effects like nausea, headaches and abdominal bloating. Only in a small proportion of cases, a condition called Ovarian Hyper-stimulation (OHSS) occurs, where there is an exaggerated ovarian response. The presentation may vary from abdominal pain, nausea, fluid collection in the body to clot formation in legs causing embolism and stroke. Such cases can be identified ahead of time but only toa limited extent. In case of severe OHSS, the patient may need intensive care by critical care specialists for which she may have to be referred to a tertiary care Centre.
  3. At times the ovarian response is poor or absent, in spite of using a high dose of drugs. Under these circumstances, the treatment cycle may be cancelled.
  4. There is no guarantee that: a. The oocytes will be retrieved in all cases. b. The oocytes will be fertilized in all cases. c. Even if there were fertilization, the resulting embryos would be of suitable quality to be transferred. All these unforeseen situations will result in the cancellation of any treatment even though there may have been significant financial loss in the course.
